Qantas Insurance: Does It Cover Rental Cars?

Home / Blog / Blog Details

When planning a trip, one of the most common concerns travelers face is whether their insurance covers rental cars. For frequent flyers with Qantas, understanding the specifics of Qantas Insurance—especially regarding rental car coverage—can save time, money, and stress. With rising travel costs, geopolitical uncertainties, and increasing demand for flexible travel options, knowing what your insurance includes is more important than ever.

Understanding Qantas Insurance

Qantas offers various insurance products tailored to different travel needs, including domestic and international travel insurance. These policies often include medical coverage, trip cancellations, lost luggage, and—depending on the plan—rental car protection. However, the extent of rental car coverage varies, and travelers must review their policy details carefully.

Types of Qantas Insurance Policies

  1. Qantas Travel Insurance – Covers medical emergencies, trip disruptions, and sometimes rental car excess.
  2. Qantas Comprehensive Car Hire Excess Insurance – Specifically designed to cover excess fees in case of rental car damage.
  3. Qantas Credit Card Insurance – Some Qantas-linked credit cards offer complimentary rental car coverage as a cardholder benefit.

Does Qantas Insurance Cover Rental Cars?

The short answer: It depends on the policy.

Standard Travel Insurance vs. Rental Car Coverage

Most standard Qantas Travel Insurance policies include some form of rental car excess coverage, but this is often limited. For example:

  • Domestic Policies – May offer minimal rental car excess coverage (e.g., up to AUD 5,000).
  • International Policies – Typically provide higher coverage limits but may exclude certain countries or vehicle types.

However, this coverage usually only applies if:

  • The rental is for personal use (not business).
  • The driver is listed on the rental agreement.
  • The rental period does not exceed a specified duration (e.g., 30 days).

Comprehensive Car Hire Excess Insurance

For frequent renters, Qantas offers a standalone Car Hire Excess Insurance policy. This covers:

  • Damage to the rental vehicle.
  • Theft of the rental car.
  • Liability for third-party property damage (in some cases).

This policy can be more cost-effective than purchasing excess coverage directly from rental companies, which often charge high daily fees.

Global Trends Impacting Rental Car Insurance

1. Rising Travel Demand Post-Pandemic

With borders reopening, travel has surged, leading to higher demand for rental cars—and consequently, higher insurance costs. Many travelers are unaware that their standard travel insurance may not fully cover rental car incidents, leading to unexpected out-of-pocket expenses.

2. Geopolitical Risks and Rental Car Restrictions

In regions with political instability (e.g., parts of the Middle East, Eastern Europe), rental car companies may impose stricter insurance requirements. Some Qantas policies exclude coverage in high-risk areas, so checking the fine print is crucial.

3. Climate Change and Natural Disasters

Extreme weather events (wildfires, floods, hurricanes) are increasing globally. If a rental car is damaged due to a natural disaster, will Qantas Insurance cover it? Most policies exclude "acts of God," meaning travelers may need additional coverage.

4. The Shift to Electric and Luxury Rentals

As more travelers opt for electric or luxury rental cars, insurance costs rise. High-value vehicles often have higher excess fees, and not all Qantas policies automatically cover them. Always verify coverage limits before renting a premium car.

How to Maximize Your Qantas Rental Car Coverage

1. Review Your Policy Before Renting

  • Check if rental car excess coverage is included.
  • Confirm coverage limits and exclusions (e.g., off-road driving, certain countries).

2. Consider Standalone Excess Insurance

If your Qantas Travel Insurance has low rental car coverage, purchasing a Qantas Comprehensive Car Hire Excess Insurance policy can be a smart move.

3. Use a Qantas-Linked Credit Card

Some premium Qantas credit cards include complimentary rental car insurance. However, these often require you to pay for the rental with the card and may have restrictions (e.g., age limits, vehicle types).

4. Compare with Rental Company Insurance

Sometimes, rental companies offer better protection, especially for high-risk scenarios (e.g., tire/windshield damage). Weigh the costs against Qantas Insurance benefits.

Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. Assuming All Rentals Are Covered

Not all vehicles are eligible—exotic cars, vans, or motorcycles may be excluded.

2. Ignoring Country-Specific Exclusions

Some policies exclude rentals in certain countries (e.g., the USA, where liability laws differ).

3. Overlooking Excess Fees

Even with coverage, you may still need to pay an excess fee upfront before claiming reimbursement.

4. Failing to Report Incidents Immediately

Most policies require immediate reporting of accidents to both the rental company and insurer. Delays can void coverage.

Final Thoughts

While Qantas Insurance can provide valuable rental car coverage, travelers must proactively verify their policy details. In today’s unpredictable travel landscape—marked by rising costs, geopolitical tensions, and climate-related risks—being underinsured can lead to significant financial losses. Whether you’re renting a car for a weekend getaway or an international road trip, taking the time to understand your Qantas Insurance coverage ensures peace of mind on the road.
