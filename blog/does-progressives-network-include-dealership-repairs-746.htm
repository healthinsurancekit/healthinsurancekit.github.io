Does Progressive’s Network Include Dealership Repairs?

Home / Blog / Blog Details

When it comes to auto insurance, one of the most common questions drivers ask is whether their policy covers repairs at dealerships. Progressive, one of the largest auto insurers in the U.S., offers a range of coverage options, but does their network include dealership repairs? The answer isn’t always straightforward, especially in today’s rapidly evolving automotive landscape.

Understanding Progressive’s Repair Network

Progressive, like many insurers, has a network of preferred repair shops. These shops are vetted for quality and often offer guarantees on their work. But what about dealerships?

The Role of Dealerships in Auto Repairs

Dealerships are often the go-to choice for drivers, especially those with newer or luxury vehicles. They employ manufacturer-trained technicians and use OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer) parts, which can be crucial for maintaining warranties and ensuring top-tier repairs.

However, dealership repairs are typically more expensive than independent shops. This raises the question: Does Progressive’s network include dealerships, or do they steer policyholders toward more cost-effective options?

Does Progressive Work with Dealerships?

The short answer is yes, but with caveats. Progressive does include some dealerships in its network, particularly those that meet their standards for quality and efficiency. However, they don’t automatically cover all dealership repairs. Here’s what you need to know:

  1. Preferred vs. Non-Preferred Shops

    • If you choose a dealership within Progressive’s network, the claims process is streamlined, and you may benefit from additional guarantees.
    • If you opt for a non-preferred dealership, you might face higher out-of-pocket costs or need to get prior approval.

  2. OEM Parts vs. Aftermarket Parts

    • Dealerships typically use OEM parts, which are identical to those installed at the factory.
    • Progressive may prefer aftermarket parts to save costs, but they often allow OEM parts if requested—especially for newer cars.

  3. Warranty Considerations

    • Some manufacturers require dealership repairs to maintain warranty validity. Progressive usually accommodates these cases.

The Bigger Picture: Why This Matters in 2024

The auto repair industry is undergoing massive changes, influenced by technology, supply chain issues, and shifting consumer expectations. Here’s why Progressive’s stance on dealership repairs is particularly relevant today:

The Rise of Electric Vehicles (EVs)

EVs require specialized knowledge and tools, often only available at dealerships. If you own a Tesla, Rivian, or another EV, dealership repairs might be non-negotiable. Progressive has had to adapt its network to include more EV-certified dealerships.

Supply Chain Challenges

Post-pandemic supply chain disruptions have made OEM parts harder to source. Some insurers, including Progressive, have had to adjust their policies, sometimes favoring aftermarket parts even at dealerships.

Consumer Rights and Transparency

More drivers are demanding transparency in repairs. States like California have laws requiring insurers to disclose whether they’re using OEM or aftermarket parts. Progressive’s approach to dealership repairs reflects broader industry trends toward accountability.

How to Ensure Dealership Repairs Are Covered

If you want your repairs done at a dealership, follow these steps:

1. Check Progressive’s Network First

  • Use their online tool or call to confirm if your preferred dealership is in-network.

2. Understand Your Policy

  • Some policies explicitly state whether dealership repairs are covered. Review your contract or ask your agent.

3. Negotiate if Necessary

  • If Progressive initially denies a dealership repair, push back—especially if it’s warranty-related.

Final Thoughts

Progressive’s network does include some dealerships, but coverage depends on multiple factors. As cars become more advanced and repair requirements stricter, insurers will need to keep evolving. For now, the key is knowing your policy, advocating for your needs, and staying informed about industry changes.

Whether you drive a gas-powered classic or a cutting-edge EV, understanding your repair options ensures you get the best service without unnecessary hassles.
