The Best Car Insurance for Drivers with Speeding Tickets

Home / Blog / Blog Details

Speeding tickets are more than just a nuisance—they can significantly impact your car insurance rates. In today’s fast-paced world, where road safety and environmental concerns are hot topics, finding affordable and reliable car insurance after a speeding violation is crucial. Whether you’re a frequent speeder or had a one-time lapse in judgment, this guide will help you navigate the best car insurance options for drivers with speeding tickets.

Why Speeding Tickets Affect Your Insurance Rates

Insurance companies view speeding tickets as a red flag. They indicate risky driving behavior, which increases the likelihood of future claims. According to recent studies, drivers with speeding tickets are 50% more likely to be involved in an accident. This risk translates into higher premiums.

How Much Will Your Rates Increase?

The exact increase depends on several factors:
- Severity of the violation – A minor speeding ticket (5-10 mph over the limit) may raise rates by 10-20%, while excessive speeding (20+ mph over) could spike premiums by 30% or more.
- State laws – Some states, like California, impose stricter penalties for speeding violations.
- Insurance provider – Companies like State Farm and Geico may be more forgiving than others.

Top Car Insurance Companies for Drivers with Speeding Tickets

Not all insurers penalize speeding tickets equally. Some specialize in high-risk drivers, offering more competitive rates. Below are the best options:

1. Geico – Best for Affordable Rates After a Ticket

Geico is known for its lenient approach to minor violations. Drivers with a single speeding ticket may see only a modest rate hike. Their online tools also make it easy to compare quotes.

Pros:
- Competitive pricing
- Discounts for safe driving after a violation
- Easy-to-use mobile app

Cons:
- Rates may still be high for multiple violations

2. State Farm – Best for Personalized Discounts

State Farm offers accident forgiveness and defensive driving discounts, which can help offset premium increases. Their agents provide personalized service, making them a solid choice for drivers looking to rebuild their record.

Pros:
- Accident forgiveness available
- Strong customer service
- Good student and defensive driving discounts

Cons:
- Slightly higher base rates than competitors

3. Progressive – Best for High-Risk Drivers

Progressive specializes in non-standard insurance, making them ideal for drivers with multiple violations. Their Snapshot program rewards safe driving habits over time.

Pros:
- Flexible payment plans
- Usage-based discounts
- High-risk driver expertise

Cons:
- Premiums can be expensive initially

4. USAA – Best for Military Members

If you qualify (active military or veteran), USAA offers some of the lowest rates, even with speeding tickets. Their customer satisfaction ratings are consistently high.

Pros:
- Exceptional customer service
- Low rates for eligible drivers
- Strong financial stability

Cons:
- Limited to military-affiliated drivers

How to Lower Your Insurance Rates After a Speeding Ticket

While your rates may increase, there are ways to mitigate the financial impact:

Take a Defensive Driving Course

Many insurers offer discounts for completing an approved course. This also demonstrates responsibility to your provider.

Shop Around Annually

Insurance rates fluctuate, and loyalty doesn’t always pay. Compare quotes every year to ensure you’re getting the best deal.

Opt for Usage-Based Insurance

Programs like Progressive’s Snapshot or Allstate’s Drivewise track your driving habits. Safe behavior can lead to significant discounts.

Increase Your Deductible

A higher deductible means lower premiums—just make sure you can afford the out-of-pocket cost if you file a claim.

The Future of Car Insurance and Speeding Violations

With advancements in telematics and AI, insurers are shifting toward real-time monitoring. Some companies now use smartphone apps to track speed, braking, and acceleration. While this raises privacy concerns, it also offers opportunities for safer drivers to save money.

Environmental Impact of Speeding

Excessive speeding isn’t just risky—it’s also bad for the environment. Faster driving increases fuel consumption and emissions. Some insurers, like Lemonade, are now offering eco-friendly discounts for low-mileage drivers.

Legislative Changes on the Horizon

Several states are reconsidering how speeding tickets affect insurance. California’s Proposition 103, for example, limits how much insurers can raise rates after minor violations. Keeping an eye on these changes can help you plan ahead.

Final Thoughts

A speeding ticket doesn’t have to break the bank. By choosing the right insurer and taking proactive steps, you can keep your premiums manageable. Whether you opt for Geico’s affordability or Progressive’s high-risk expertise, the key is to stay informed and drive safely moving forward.
