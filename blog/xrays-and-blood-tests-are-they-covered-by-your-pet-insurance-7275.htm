X-Rays and Blood Tests: Are They Covered by Your Pet Insurance?

Home / Blog / Blog Details

Pets are more than just animals—they’re family. As pet owners, we want to ensure they receive the best possible care, especially when they’re sick or injured. Diagnostic tools like X-rays and blood tests are essential for veterinarians to accurately diagnose and treat health issues. But here’s the big question: Does your pet insurance cover these procedures?

The Rising Cost of Pet Healthcare

Veterinary care has become increasingly advanced—and expensive. A simple X-ray can cost anywhere from $100 to $300, while blood tests can range from $50 to $200, depending on the complexity. For chronic conditions or emergencies, these costs can quickly add up, leaving pet owners with hefty bills.

Why Pet Insurance Matters

Pet insurance helps mitigate these expenses, but coverage varies widely. Some policies cover diagnostics like X-rays and blood tests, while others exclude them or require additional riders. Understanding your policy’s fine print is crucial to avoiding unexpected out-of-pocket costs.

What Does Pet Insurance Typically Cover?

Most pet insurance plans fall into three categories:

  1. Accident-Only Plans – Covers injuries like broken bones (which often require X-rays) but not illnesses.
  2. Accident & Illness Plans – Includes diagnostics for both injuries and diseases.
  3. Wellness Plans – Optional add-ons for routine care, such as annual bloodwork.

Are X-Rays Covered?

X-rays are commonly covered under Accident & Illness plans, especially if they’re deemed medically necessary. For example:
- Trauma cases (e.g., car accidents, fractures)
- Foreign object ingestion (e.g., a dog swallowing a toy)
- Chronic conditions (e.g., arthritis monitoring)

However, some insurers may require pre-authorization or impose limits on the number of X-rays per year.

Are Blood Tests Covered?

Blood tests are often covered under Accident & Illness plans when used for diagnosing or monitoring conditions like:
- Diabetes
- Kidney disease
- Infections

Wellness plans may cover routine bloodwork (e.g., annual checkups), but these are usually add-ons, not part of standard coverage.

Common Exclusions and Limitations

Even if your policy covers diagnostics, watch out for:
- Pre-existing conditions – Most insurers won’t cover diagnostics related to a pet’s prior health issues.
- Breed-specific exclusions – Some breeds are prone to conditions that require frequent testing, which may not be fully covered.
- Annual or lifetime caps – Policies may limit how much they’ll pay for diagnostics each year.

How to Choose the Right Pet Insurance

1. Compare Multiple Providers

Look at companies like Trupanion, Healthy Paws, and Nationwide to see how they handle diagnostic coverage.

2. Read the Fine Print

Check for:
- Reimbursement rates (e.g., 70%, 80%, or 90%)
- Deductibles (annual vs. per-incident)
- Waiting periods (some policies make you wait 14–30 days before coverage kicks in)

3. Ask About Add-Ons

If your pet is older or has a breed-specific risk, consider a wellness rider for routine blood tests.

Real-Life Scenarios: When Coverage Makes a Difference

Case 1: Emergency X-Ray After a Fall

A dog jumps off the couch and limps. The vet recommends an X-ray, which reveals a fractured leg. With insurance covering 80% of the $250 X-ray, the owner pays only $50 out of pocket.

Case 2: Blood Test for a Sick Cat

A cat shows signs of lethargy and vomiting. Bloodwork ($150) diagnoses kidney disease. An Accident & Illness plan reimburses 90%, saving the owner $135.

Final Thoughts

Pet insurance can be a financial lifesaver, but not all policies are created equal. If X-rays and blood tests are important to you (and they should be!), take the time to research and select a plan that offers comprehensive diagnostic coverage. Your furry friend’s health—and your wallet—will thank you.
