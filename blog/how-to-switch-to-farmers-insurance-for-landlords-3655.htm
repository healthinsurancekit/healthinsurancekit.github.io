How to Switch to Farmers Insurance for Landlords

Home / Blog / Blog Details

The real estate market is more volatile than ever, with rising interest rates, inflation, and unpredictable tenant behaviors making landlord insurance a critical safety net. If you’re a property owner, you know that standard homeowners’ insurance won’t cut it—landlords need specialized coverage. Farmers Insurance has long been a trusted name in the industry, offering tailored policies for rental properties. Here’s how to make the switch seamlessly while addressing today’s biggest challenges.

Why Farmers Insurance Stands Out for Landlords

Comprehensive Coverage for Modern Risks

From climate-related disasters to increasing liability claims, landlords face unprecedented risks. Farmers Insurance offers:
- Property damage protection (including wildfires, floods, and vandalism)
- Loss of rental income coverage (critical in an era of rising vacancies)
- Liability insurance (essential as tenant lawsuits become more common)

Competitive Pricing in a High-Cost Economy

With inflation driving up repair and replacement costs, Farmers provides flexible payment options and discounts for:
- Bundling multiple properties
- Installing security systems
- Maintaining claim-free records

Step-by-Step Guide to Switching to Farmers Insurance

1. Assess Your Current Policy’s Gaps

Before making the switch, review your existing policy to identify:
- Coverage limits: Are they enough to rebuild or repair in today’s market?
- Exclusions: Does your policy exclude short-term rentals (e.g., Airbnb)?
- Cost inefficiencies: Are you overpaying for outdated coverage?

2. Get a Customized Farmers Insurance Quote

Farmers’ online tools and local agents can help you:
- Input property details (location, age, occupancy type)
- Select add-ons like umbrella liability or ordinance/law coverage
- Compare quotes side-by-side with your current insurer

3. Time the Switch Strategically

Avoid lapses in coverage by:
- Aligning with your renewal date (most insurers allow mid-term cancellations)
- Confirming Farmers’ effective start date before canceling the old policy

4. Notify Tenants and Stakeholders

Ensure a smooth transition by:
- Updating lease agreements with new insurer contact info
- Informing property managers (if applicable)

5. Leverage Farmers’ Landlord-Specific Resources

Farmers offers:
- 24/7 claims service (critical for emergencies like burst pipes)
- Risk mitigation tips (e.g., preventing mold or tenant injuries)

Addressing Today’s Top Landlord Concerns

Climate Change and Property Resilience

With hurricanes, wildfires, and floods intensifying, Farmers’ policies now include:
- Green rebuild options (e.g., eco-friendly materials)
- Extended replacement cost coverage (for sudden price surges)

Tenant Screening in a Tight Rental Market

Farmers partners with services to help landlords:
- Run background checks (reducing risks of non-payment or property damage)
- Access legal advice (for eviction moratorium complexities)

Cybersecurity for Rental Businesses

As digital payments and smart home tech rise, Farmers covers:
- Data breaches (if tenant info is compromised)
- Smart device failures (e.g., hacked security systems)

Final Tips for a Hassle-Free Transition

  • Document property conditions before switching (photos/videos)
  • Ask about loyalty rewards (Farmers discounts long-term clients)
  • Re-evaluate annually (adjust coverage as property values or laws change)

Switching to Farmers Insurance isn’t just about better rates—it’s about future-proofing your rental business in an unpredictable world. Whether you own a single duplex or a multi-unit portfolio, now’s the time to prioritize protection.
